# Interview Story Bank

Use careful, truthful phrasing: supported, coordinated, tracked, helped maintain, contributed to, used structured documentation, identified barriers, improved visibility. Do not invent outcomes or metrics.

## 70-80 Client Caseload

- Situation: Current behavioral health role involves a high-volume adult client caseload.
- Task: Keep follow-ups, service needs, documentation, and barriers organized.
- Action: Used structured documentation, follow-up prioritization, client/family communication, and coordination with internal/external providers.
- Result: Helped maintain continuity of care and visibility into client needs while balancing competing priorities.

## Higher-Acuity TSU Clients

- Situation: Prior TSU work involved approximately 30 higher-acuity crisis/LOC 5 clients.
- Task: Support stabilization and transitions while maintaining careful documentation.
- Action: Coordinated follow-ups, resources, provider communication, and escalation when barriers or risks appeared.
- Result: Built experience with high-need behavioral health workflows and crisis-adjacent coordination.

## Transitioning Clients To Community Support

- Situation: Clients needed support moving from discharge or transitional services into community-based care.
- Task: Help connect them with outpatient services and practical supports.
- Action: Coordinated appointments, medication access, benefits, housing, food, and follow-up resources.
- Result: Helped reduce gaps between discharge planning and community support.

## ANSA/Reassessment Workflows

- Situation: Reassessments and ANSA-related workflows required timing, accuracy, and documentation discipline.
- Task: Track reassessment needs and keep records current.
- Action: Used structured tracking and documentation habits to support reassessment timing and follow-up.
- Result: Helped maintain audit-ready records and service continuity.

## Multidisciplinary Coordination

- Situation: Behavioral health needs often required coordination across multiple providers.
- Task: Keep care plans moving across psychiatrists, therapists, ACT, MAT/SUD programs, clinics, pharmacies, and community agencies.
- Action: Communicated barriers, next steps, and service needs while documenting follow-up.
- Result: Strengthened cross-functional coordination and stakeholder communication skills.

## Resource Navigation

- Situation: Clients often faced practical barriers around benefits, food, housing, rent, utilities, or Social Security.
- Task: Help identify and connect clients to appropriate resources.
- Action: Shared resource information, coordinated referrals, and followed up on service needs.
- Result: Supported access to practical resources that can affect care continuity.

## Documentation And Compliance

- Situation: Behavioral health, special education, and ABA work all required accurate records.
- Task: Maintain timely, confidential, and audit-aware documentation.
- Action: Prepared service notes, follow-up records, progress documentation, and structured data where appropriate.
- Result: Built a strong documentation discipline that transfers to healthcare operations, QI, and research roles.

## Tracking Caseload/Service Needs

- Situation: High-volume service work required visibility into who needed what next.
- Task: Track needs, barriers, appointments, reassessments, and follow-up priorities.
- Action: Used organized notes, queues, and Excel-style tracking habits.
- Result: Improved personal visibility into next actions and operational needs.

## IT Helpdesk Troubleshooting

- Task: Resolve or route issues while communicating clearly.
- Action: Documented cases, troubleshot common issues, supported tools including SPSS, and escalated when needed.
- Result: Built technical support, customer service, and issue-documentation experience.

## Special Education Case Management

- Situation: Special education work required case management, family communication, and compliance-heavy documentation.
- Task: Manage student supports, documentation cycles, and stakeholder communication.
- Action: Tracked accommodations, progress, family communication, and reporting needs.
- Result: Strengthened case management and regulated documentation skills.

## ABA/RBT Behavioral Data Experience

- Situation: ABA work required structured observation and behavioral data collection.
- Task: Record behavior-related information accurately and communicate useful context.
- Action: Collected high-frequency behavioral data and prepared detailed session notes.
- Result: Built careful observation and data-recording habits relevant to research and clinical documentation.

- Situation: Juan is building analytics credibility while working in behavioral health.
